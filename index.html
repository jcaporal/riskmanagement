3. Analyzing Risk: Score & Strategy Mandates

Before choosing a plan, we need to quickly score the risk. Use these criteria to determine your risk's priority level. The score tells you exactly what level of action is mandated.

üéØ Impact Scoring (1-5)

5‚Äî Goal impossible; client loss or major breach.
4‚Äî Severe challenge; major delay or significant financial penalty.
3‚Äî Noticeable impact; minor schedule delay or budget overrun (5-10%).
2‚Äî Minimal disruption; absorbed within current team capacity.
1‚Äî No measurable effect on project.

‚è±Ô∏è Probability Scoring (1-5)

5‚Äî Almost certain; >80% chance (or has started).
4‚Äî Likely; 60%-80% chance based on trends/history.
3‚Äî Possible; 40%-60% chance; a toss-up.
2‚Äî Unlikely; 20%-40% chance; specific conditions must align.
1‚Äî Rare; <20% chance; theoretical but unlikely to occur.

Your Playbook Actions: The Strategy List

Once you know your mandate (Red, Yellow, or Green), select the precise strategy. The goal is simple: Build stability and maximize team potential.

üõ°Ô∏è Stability Moves (Threats)

Avoid: Eliminate the risk source. Action Example: Remove a non-compliant service vendor immediately.
Mitigate: Shrink Impact/Probability. Action Example: Launch cross-training to reduce reliance on one expert.
Transfer: Shift consequences to a third party. Action Example: Buy insurance or include a penalty clause in a contract.
Accept: Take no action, but monitor. Action Example: Document the risk and assign an owner for weekly checks.

üöÄ Growth Moves (Opportunities)

Exploit: Commit resources to guarantee the benefit. Action Example: Immediately redirect unplanned capacity to build a new feature prototype.
Enhance: Increase the likelihood or size of the benefit. Action Example: Invest in new training to boost team capability linked to the opportunity.
Share: Collaborate to maximize collective benefits. Action Example: Form a joint venture with a trusted partner to leverage market entry costs.
Accept: Be prepared to implement if it occurs. Action Example: Document a high-level plan, but save resources for now.

1. Defining Risk: Gaining Clarity

🎯 Event

The specific uncertain situation that might happen. It names WHAT we’re facing, moving us from worry to action.

📉 Impact

The size of the consequence on our goals. It determines the magnitude of stability or growth that is truly at stake.

⏳ Probability

The likelihood of the event actually happening. It tells you the urgency—where to focus your energy.

🔻 Threat (Negative)

An event that, if it occurs, will have a detrimental impact on our objectives (e.g., staff attrition spikes).

🔺 Opportunity (Positive)

An event that, if it occurs, will have a beneficial impact on our objectives (e.g., a new process cuts delivery time in half).

Action Item: To effectively manage uncertainty, both Threats and Opportunities must be identified and defined.

3. Analyzing Risk: Score & Strategy Mandates

Before choosing a plan, we need to quickly score the risk. Use these criteria to determine your risk's priority level. The score tells you exactly what level of action is mandated.

🎯 Impact Scoring (1-5)

5Goal impossible; client loss or major breach.
4Severe challenge; major delay or significant financial penalty.
3Noticeable impact; minor schedule delay or budget overrun (5-10%).
2Minimal disruption; absorbed within current team capacity.
1No measurable effect on project.

⏱️ Probability Scoring (1-5)

5Almost certain; >80% chance (or has started).
4Likely; 60%-80% chance based on trends/history.
3Possible; 40%-60% chance; a toss-up.
2Unlikely; 20%-40% chance; specific conditions must align.
1Rare; <20% chance; theoretical but unlikely to occur.

How the Score Works:

The matrix shows that your mandate is driven by the product of Probability x Impact:

  • Score ≤ 3 (Green Zone): Track & Monitor mandate (ACCEPT). Low risk, low commitment needed.
  • Score 4–15 (Yellow Zone): Active Attention mandate (MITIGATE/ENHANCE). Requires a defined plan and resources.
  • Score ≥ 16 (Red Zone): HIGH PRIORITY mandate (AVOID/EXPLOIT). Requires aggressive, immediate action.

Your Playbook Actions: The Strategy List

Once you know your mandate (Red, Yellow, or Green), select the precise strategy. The goal is simple: Build stability and maximize team potential.

🛡️ Stability Moves (Threats)

Avoid: Eliminate the risk source. Action Example: Remove a non-compliant service vendor immediately.
Mitigate: Shrink Impact/Probability. Action Example: Launch cross-training to reduce reliance on one expert.
Transfer: Shift consequences to a third party. Action Example: Buy insurance or include a penalty clause in a contract.
Accept: Take no action, but monitor. Action Example: Document the risk and assign an owner for weekly checks.

🚀 Growth Moves (Opportunities)

Exploit: Commit resources to guarantee the benefit. Action Example: Immediately redirect unplanned capacity to build a new feature prototype.
Enhance: Increase the likelihood or size of the benefit. Action Example: Invest in new training to boost team capability linked to the opportunity.
Share: Collaborate to maximize collective benefits. Action Example: Form a joint venture with a trusted partner to leverage market entry costs.
Accept: Be prepared to implement if it occurs. Action Example: Document a high-level plan, but save resources for now.
